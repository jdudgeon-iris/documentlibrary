Home Safety Checklist
Item Status/Date Plan Follow Up
The yard is free of broken glass, trash, debris, and animal feces
Walkways are free of debris and in good repair
Entrance is free of clutter and debris
Trash is kept in covered containers and/or bagged
Screen doors have guards and/or locks
Doors have locks (out of reach of children, age dependent)
There are 2 easily accessible exits (at least 1 door and 1 window) free of debris in separate parts of the home
Safety gates at top/bottom of stairs
Basement doors are locked (or have safety gate)
Space heaters are out of reach of children, and are not near furniture, curtains, or trash cans
The fireplace has a cover
The second floor has a window ladder for escape and/or plan of escape from 2nd story
Family has emergency plans for fires that includes a meeting spot
Family has an emergency plan for tornados
Home has operational smoke/CO detectors on every level
Emergency phone numbers are posted in/near the phone; children know 911
Lighters/matches/ashtrays are kept out of reach of children
Has a fire extinguisher and is out of reach of children
Windows are not broken or boarded
Opening windows have screens
Blind and drapery cords are secured and out of reach
Windows are not opened more than 4 inches or have guards
Hallways and stairways are well lit
Unused outlets have outlet covers or are inaccessible to small children
Outlets and cords are not overloaded
Electrical cords are in good condition and are kept out of reach of children
Home has a heat source
Home has ventilation (air conditioning or windows that can open)
Fans have guards and are out of reach of children
All medications have child-proof lids and are out of reach of children
All purses are out of reach of children
Houseplants are kept out of reach of small children
Houses build before 1978 are tested for lead
Home has running water or access to water
Basement is free of debris or standing water
There is a working toilet (or outhouse as appropriate)
There is hot water (under 120° F) or a means to heat it
Infants and toddlers are not left in tubs/pools/bathrooms unsupervised
Heavy appliances are on a broad basis to avoid tipping
Scissors, knives and other sharp objects are kept out of reach of children
Hot pans are kept to the back of the stove and handles turn to the side
All cleaning products are stored in locked cabinets or out of children’s reach
Tools are stored in locked areas or kept out of children’s reach
Safe sleeping practices are used for infants/toddlers
All children have their own appropriate sleeping space
Sleepwear and bedding are seasonally and age appropriate
No bumper pads, pillows, soft bedding or stuffed animals in crib
Toys are age appropriate
Toys are checked for loose parts, sharp edges, or small parts – broke toys are thrown away
Car seats are age/weight appropriate and installed correctly
Infants/children are not left unattended in vehicles, especially in warmer weather
Litter boxes/ cages are out of reach of children
If animals are kept indoors, floors are free of feces
Home is free of pests (rodents/bugs)
There is a three-day food supply in the home
Home is free of structural concerns and other health hazards
Breakable items are kept out of reach of children
Guns have locks or stored in locked cabinets. Ammunition is stored separately and out of the reach of children
Floors are free of small debris which can be placed in toddler’s mouth
Food given to children are cut into small non-choke able pieces
Infants are not left alone on changing tables, beds, sofas, or chairs
Parents keep hot liquids out of reach of children and do not hold children while carrying hot liquids
Check frequently for product recalls on toys, food, furniture, etc.
